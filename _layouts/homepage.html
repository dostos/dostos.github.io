I'm a Ph.D. student at the Human-Centered Computer Systems Lab under the supervision of professor Youngki Lee. I build a software platform for edge devices that enable real-time mixed reality applications for everyone.

My research journey began in game engine development at DigiPen Institute of Technology, where I built physics engines and shipped titles including an E3 College Game Competition Finalist. After working on Minecraft: Education Edition at Microsoft, I shifted to medical imaging and 3D vision during my M.S. at Seoul National University, publishing in IEEE TMI and other venues. These threads — real-time systems, 3D understanding, and on-device intelligence — converge in my current Ph.D. work on mixed-reality and spatial computing.
